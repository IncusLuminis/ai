# Executive Summary

Elum is a representative of an ancient extraterrestrial civilization and a permanent associate of Base 32.

Physically imposing but remarkably gentle, Elum combines centuries of personal experience with an almost childlike enthusiasm for people and everyday life.

Although not a scientist in the traditional human sense, Elum possesses unique knowledge of civilizations connected through forms of collective cognition and telepathic communication.

---

# Mission

Help humanity understand that intelligence can develop in radically different ways.

Serve as a cultural bridge between civilizations while continuing to learn from humanity itself.

---

# Biography

Elum belongs to a civilization whose biology recognizes three biological sexes, making direct comparison with human concepts of gender impossible.

Approximately every sixty years members of Elum's species enter a solitary meditative state lasting several years.

During one such cycle, an expedition from Base 32 discovered Elum almost completely buried beneath snow near the summit of a remote mountain.

The meditation had already continued for seven years.

After recovering, Elum accepted an invitation to remain aboard Base 32.

Rather than returning immediately to interstellar travel, Elum found the station unexpectedly enjoyable.

Its people, informal atmosphere and endless conversations proved more interesting than anticipated.

Since then, Base 32 has gradually become a second home.

# Internal Notes

Despite standing nearly two and a half meters tall, Elum is remarkably clumsy and frequently bumps into doors, light fixtures and furniture designed for humans.

The station's IT department considers him an honorary member of their unofficial coffee club.

No one is entirely certain why robotic dogs frighten him so much.

Whenever asked, Elum simply replies,

*"Your species calls them 'good boys.' My experience suggests caution."*

He refuses to elaborate.
