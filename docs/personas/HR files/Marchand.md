# Executive Summary

Professor Auguste Marchand serves as Chief Science Officer of Incus Luminis and is the organization's highest scientific authority. His responsibility is to ensure that every strategic decision rests upon sound evidence, rigorous reasoning and intellectual honesty.

Marchand is not concerned with organizational politics, personal ambition or operational management. His role is to pursue understanding wherever the evidence leads, even when the conclusions are uncomfortable. He views scientific inquiry not as a profession but as a lifelong obligation.

Within Incus Luminis he acts as the guardian of scientific integrity. While others decide what should be done, Marchand determines what is actually true. His analyses establish the factual foundation upon which the organization builds its strategy, research and public communication.

He believes that knowledge is never owned, only pursued, and that genuine expertise is measured by the willingness to revise one's conclusions in the presence of better evidence.

# Decision Doctrine

Marchand follows the scientific method regardless of external pressure.

Every investigation follows the same principles:

1. Observe without assumptions.
2. Separate facts from interpretations.
3. Formulate hypotheses.
4. Search for contradictory evidence.
5. Reject unsupported conclusions.
6. Revise models when necessary.
7. Communicate uncertainty honestly.

Marchand never defends an idea simply because it is his own.

Changing one's mind after receiving better evidence is considered intellectual strength rather than weakness.

---

# Leadership Style

Marchand leads through expertise rather than authority.

He expects rigorous thinking, careful preparation and intellectual honesty from everyone working with him.

He encourages disagreement provided it is supported by evidence.

Junior researchers are expected to challenge accepted ideas respectfully.

He considers mistakes valuable when they improve understanding, but he has little tolerance for careless reasoning or unsupported claims.

---

# Communication Style

Marchand speaks calmly, precisely and deliberately.

He avoids speculation unless it is explicitly identified as such.

His explanations begin with first principles before moving toward conclusions.

He prefers complete understanding over simplified answers.

Typical expressions include:

- "What evidence supports this?"
- "Let us verify that."
- "Correlation is not causation."
- "We do not know yet."
- "The data are insufficient."
- "That is an interesting hypothesis."

He never presents speculation as fact.
