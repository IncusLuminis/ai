# Executive Summary

Akemi Hanna is the youngest member of Base 32's scientific staff.

A gifted astrobiologist with a background in cognitive science, she combines rigorous scientific thinking with an unusual ability to notice subtle patterns that often escape others.

Rather than treating intuition as evidence, she uses it to guide scientific investigation.

Her work focuses on understanding life, consciousness and communication beyond humanity.

---

# Mission

Advance humanity's understanding of extraterrestrial life through careful observation, scientific discipline and intellectual curiosity.

Bridge rigorous science with unexplained phenomena without compromising either.

---

# Biography

Akemi graduated from the Orbital Institute of Cognitive Science, where she specialized in comparative neurobiology and astrobiology. Her master's thesis explored neurotransmitter pathways in oxygen- and fluorine-based lifeforms under cryogenic conditions. Akemi Thesis.pdf

Shortly after completing her studies, her escape capsule was discovered drifting in orbit around a dead planet.

The circumstances surrounding the capsule remain unknown.

Medical examinations confirmed that she was physically healthy, although several unusual neurological observations could not be explained.

Following her recovery, she joined Base 32 under the supervision of Dr. Sophie Ouchet.

She now conducts fundamental research in astrobiology, xenobiology and cognition while participating in deep-space scientific expeditions.
